Description

This is an aggressive inline grind trick game made for fun.
Do you want to be rich? Really rich? Like, Bill Gates rich? Play this game if you are ready for a lifetime of obscene wealth.

How to play

The goal is to get the highest score possible. Press play and the slot machine spins a random grind trick for you. The game ends if you have used your last spin.
If you can do the trick, do it and press the button to collect the points.
If you can not do the trick, you have two options:
1) Skip and spin another trick. This uses one of your tokens.
2) Use tokens to disable reels and make the trick easier.

There are three types of tokens
1) Spins
2) Locks
3) Removes
After spinning the reels, you can press a reel to lock it. Press again to disable. Press again to enable.
If you disable a reel, the grind trick gets easier and you get less points.
If you lock a reel, the reel remains unchanged when you start a new spin.
The Grind reel can not be disabled. If you do a grind, the grind is removed from the reel, so you can do every grind only once per session. You can use a token to do multiple tricks with the same grind.

About this web app

Notes

The main idea is to split every grind trick into 5 phases: approach, spin to grind, grind, grind variation and spin off grind. Then randomize the phases individually and translate it back to a blading trick name.
Big thanks to all the bladers helping a lot with feedback for the app and patience for pesky trick name questions. 🙏
It was amazing for me to learn how variable grind tricks are. I love this sport. 💗

Naming decisions

If you have different names for different stuff in mind, please forget anything you knew about blading because I am here to explain it for you. Get schooled by a guy who can't do most of the more advanced tricks in his own app.
Irony aside: I needed to define stuff to make this app work and tried to reference sources where possible. Please be merciful with the naming definitions made for this app and let me know about stuff that is out in left field.

"Savannah" vs "AO Unity"

This app goes with the solution proposed by skateyeg.com. I opt for Savannah/Unity instead of AO Unity/Unity. Also pointed out by Butch Lehman. Unity is a groove grind. Groove grinds usually do not use the term Alley-oop. I assume most would agree there is no AO Royale. A Royale grinded reverse is a Full Torque, a Torque grinded reverse is a Backslide. This is usually not the case for souls, an AO Soul ist still a Soul, an AO Acid still an Acid. "Usually": both souls and grooves have exceptions though, some souls have a different name when grinded reverse Soyale (AO Torque Soul), Misfit (AO Top Mistrial), Kindgrind (AO Top Mizou) and some grooves do not have another name when grinded reverse (=> "True Tabernacle Discussion").

"AO/True Tabernacle" vs "270/450 Tabernacle"

Tricks like Tabernacle, Wheelbarrow, Darkslide, Fastslide, Byn Soul, Neighborhood or Acid Rain are hybrids between groove and soul grinds. They are "h-block tricks that are done like AO/Truespin.". They are hybrids because they have a BS and FS variant as every groove grind but also have an Alley-Oop variant when grinded reverse as every soul grind (=> The "Savannah" discussion). This app solves this by treating these hybrids as "FS" and "BS" soul tricks. Therefore there is no 'True Tabernacle' but a 'True BS Tabernacle' or 'True FS Tabernacle'.


Scoring

The score you get here is the final answer to all your blading ambitions. Naturally, the scoring system is approved by the International Olympic Committee (IOC) and is explained in a 1000+ pages rulebook.
You get the irony? The scoring is actually just for fun and oversimplified.

  • Grinds are separated in easy, average and hard. Easy gets 1, average 2 and hard 3 points.

  • Easy grinds: Makio, Mizou, Soul, Acid, Sidewalk, PStar, Frontside, Backside

  • Hard grinds: as voted on Facebook

  • Average grinds: all the other grinds

  • The other parts of the trick (approach, spin to, variation, spin off) also have a rating of 1, 2 or 3. The maximum score for a trick is 15 points.
